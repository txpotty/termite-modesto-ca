Termite Activity – The Silent Destructors

Termites are notorious for the devastating damage they can cause, often without being seen. They feed on cellulose, the primary component of wood, within the structure of your home. The signs of termites can be subtle and often mistaken for other issues.

Identifying Termite Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, which are small tunnels made of soil and wood, to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home). Finding these tubes on foundation walls, crawl spaces, or along wooden structures is a strong indicator of subterranean termite activity. We locate and treat these tubes, disrupting their pathways and targeting the colony.

Discovering Damaged Wood

Wood damaged by termites often has a hollowed-out appearance or can be easily probed. Drywood termites, in particular, can infest wooden furniture, beams, and walls. You might notice bubbling or peeling paint, distorted wood, or small pinholes in the surface. Our inspections meticulously check for such damage, and our localized treatments effectively eliminate drywood colonies within infested timber.

Spotting Discarded Termite Wings

During certain times of the year, often in the spring, termite swarmers (reproductive termites) emerge from the colony to mate and establish new colonies. They shed their wings shortly after swarming. Finding small piles of discarded wings, often near windowsills, doorways, or light sources, is a clear sign of a nearby termite colony and indicates the need for immediate professional assessment.

Noticing Termite Frass

Frass, or termite droppings, resembles tiny pellets and can be a sign of drywood termite activity. These termites push their fecal pellets out of small kick-out holes in the wood they are infesting. Finding small piles of what looks like sawdust or tiny grains is a strong indication of an infestation that requires specialized drywood termite treatment.
